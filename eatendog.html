Super premium dog food

The best choice for pet owners is a dog food that contains essential calories, minerals, vitamins and nutrients so that it can be used as a complete diet. Is it possible? Yes, if you choose wisely. Good options can be found among mid-price products, but many still prefer to buy super premium dog food. Let's tell you why.

1.This dog food contains predominantly animal protein rather than plant protein. It gives your pet those amino acids that are not synthesized in the body. Vegetable protein contains either very few such substances or none at all.

2.Meat accounts for at least 40% of the ingredients. This explains the higher cost compared to premium dog foods. Price is not always an indicator of quality, but in this case this rule is indeed fair. Manufacturers use different types of meat: chicken, lamb, beef, rabbit. The benefits of such a diet are obvious. Moreover, the more diverse the meat composition, the greater the nutritional value.

3.Super premium food contains a minimum of grains. There are also those where grains are completely absent. Your four-legged friends are carnivores by nature; foods such as soy, corn, and grains are not digestible by their bodies. At the same time, manufacturers sometimes introduce them into the diet, thereby reducing its cost.

4.The choice of super premium dog food is usually very large. The manufacturer has formulations for different breeds, adult pets and puppies, animals with different physiological characteristics.

How to choose?

*Offal. There are some useful and some not very useful. Manufacturers add them to replace artificial additives - this is a good thing. From offal the pet receives some microelements and vitamins that are not contained or are present in small quantities in muscle meat. For example, it is cartilage tissue, ligaments and bones that contain chondroitin and glucosamine, which are necessary for the functioning of animal joints (especially in old age). There is also a downside. The digestibility of such protein is lower; dogs do not digest it well, which can cause diarrhea.

*Dehydrated meat. This is meat that has undergone special processing to reduce the amount of moisture, and eventually became meat meal - this is how manufacturers increase its content in super premium and some premium feeds.

*Thermox. It is an antioxidant found in dog foods. It protects fats and fat-soluble vitamins from oxidation, preserves the quality of meat/fish meal, thereby increasing the shelf life of the feed. No harm to the pet's health or side effects from the thermox were detected

*Propyl gallate (E310). This is an artificial preservative that prevents fat oxidation. It is not recommended to purchase dog food that contains it. It can cause allergies, kidney problems, and stomach irritation. The preservative is prohibited in the production of baby food.

*Taurine is an important feed component. It is beneficial for the cardiac system. In dog food, its content is lower than in cat food (the need for it is lower), but its presence is highly desirable.
